Cluj-Napoca, commonly known as Cluj, is the fourth most populous city in Romania,[5] and the seat of Cluj County in the northwestern part of the country.

Sibiu is one of the most important cultural centres of Romania and was designated the European Capital of Culture for the year 2007, along with the city of Luxembourg.

Targu Mures is the seat of Mureș County, it is the 16th largest Romanian city. It lies on the Mureș river, the second longest river in Romania

Alba Iulia is a city located on the Mureş River. Since the High Middle Ages, the city has been the seat of Transylvania's Roman Catholic diocese.

Brasov, the city is notable for being an important centre of Transylvanian Saxons in the past, and a large commercial hub on the trade roads between East and West.

Deva is the capital of Hunedoara County. Its name was first recorded in 1269 as castrum Dewa.

Bistrita is the capital city of Bistrița-Năsăud County, in northern Transylvania, Romania. It is situated on the Bistrița River and the town was named after the river

Odorheiu Secuiesc is the second largest city in Harghita County.The city 95.8% are ethnic Hungarians, making it the urban settlement with the third-highest proportion of Hungarians in Romania.

Miercurea Ciuc is the county seat of Harghita County. It lies in the Székely Land, a mainly Hungarian-speaking ethno-cultural region in eastern Transylvania, and is situated in the Olt River valley.

Sfantu Gheorghe is one of the oldest cities in Transylvania, the settlement first having been documented in 1332. The city takes its name from Saint George, the patron of the local church.

Turda is a city and Municipality in Cluj County, Romania, situated on the Arieș River.

Sebes the city itself was built by German settlers - later referred as Transylvanian Saxons, but actually originating from the region of Rhine and Moselle
